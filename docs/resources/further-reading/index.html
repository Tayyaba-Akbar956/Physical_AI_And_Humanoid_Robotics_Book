Further Reading: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

This section provides additional resources, advanced topics, and specialized readings that complement and expand upon the content in the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook. These resources are organized by topic and difficulty level to guide continued learning and research.

Core Robotics Theory and Algorithms​

Advanced Control Theory​

  1. Slotine, J. J. E., & Li, W. (1991). "Applied Nonlinear Control." Prentice Hall.

    • Essential for understanding advanced control techniques for physical systems
    • Covers Lyapunov stability, adaptive control, and robust control
    • Critical for humanoid robot balance and manipulation

  2. Sage, H. G., De Mathelin, M. F., & Ostertag, E. C. (1999). "Robust control of robot manipulators: a survey." International Journal of Control, 72(14), 1332-1355.

    • Comprehensive review of robust control methods for robots
    • Essential for understanding how to handle uncertainties in physical systems

  3. Sciavicco, L., & Siciliano, B. (2000). "Modelling and Control of Robot Manipulators." Springer.

    • Advanced treatment of robot dynamics and control
    • Covers kinematics, dynamics, and control of manipulator arms

Motion Planning and Navigation​

  1. LaValle, S. M. (2006). "Planning Algorithms." Cambridge University Press.

    • Comprehensive treatment of motion planning algorithms
    • Essential for understanding path planning, configuration spaces, and sampling-based methods

  2. Choset, H., et al. (2005). "Principles of Robot Motion: Theory, Algorithms, and Implementations." MIT Press.

    • Detailed coverage of robot motion planning and navigation
    • Includes coverage of coverage path planning, dynamic environments, and multi-robot systems

  3. Kumar, V., Lynch, K., & Sastry, S. (Eds.). (2017). "Handbook of Modern Sensors: Physics, Designs, and Applications." Springer.

    • Comprehensive resource on sensors for robotics applications
    • Covers sensing principles critical for Physical AI systems

Dynamics and Mechanics​

  1. Murray, R. M., Li, Z., & Sastry, S. S. (1994). "A Mathematical Introduction to Robotic Manipulation." CRC Press.

    • Mathematical foundations of robot mechanics
    • Essential for understanding the kinematics and dynamics of robotic systems

  2. Featherstone, R. (2008). "Rigid Body Dynamics Algorithms." Springer.

    • Advanced algorithms for rigid body dynamics computation
    • Critical for accurate simulation and control of complex robotic systems

Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence​

Embodiment and Cognition​

  1. Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). "How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence." MIT Press.

    • Foundational text on how physical embodiment shapes intelligence
    • Critical for understanding Physical AI philosophy and approaches

  2. Pfeifer, R., Lungarella, M., & Iida, F. (2007). "Self-organization, embodiment, and biologically inspired robotics." Science, 318(5853), 1088-1093.

    • Biological inspiration in robotics design
    • Understanding how natural systems inspire Physical AI

  3. Ziemke, T., Jirenhed, L. A., & Winfield, A. (2005). "A constructive empiricist approach to the cognition of humanoid robots." In Proceedings of the ICRA Workshop on Automated Planning for Assistive and Rehabilitation Robotics.

    • Cognitive approaches to humanoid robot control
    • Understanding perception-action coupling

Sensorimotor Learning​

  1. Marblestone, A. L., Wayne, G., & Kording, K. P. (2016). "Toward an integration of deep learning and neuroscience." Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience, 10, 94.

    • Neural computation and learning in physical systems
    • Understanding how biological and artificial systems learn from sensorimotor experience

  2. Nguyen, A., Clune, J., Lipson, H., Stanley, K. O., & Yosinski, J. (2018). "The surprising creativity of digital evolution." Artificial Life, 26(2), 206-232.

    • Evolutionary approaches to robot learning and adaptation
    • Understanding how embodied systems can evolve behaviors

Advanced Simulation and Physics​

Physics Simulation and Modeling​

  1. Teschner, M., Heidelberger, B., Müller, M., & Gross, M. (2003). "A versatile and robust model for geometrically complex deformable solids." Proceedings Computer Graphics International.

    • Advanced physics modeling for simulation
    • Critical for realistic simulation of complex interactions

  2. Bender, J., Müller, M., & Gross, M. (2015). "Position-based simulation methods in computer graphics." Eurographics 2015.

    • Modern approaches to physics simulation
    • Understanding constraints and collision handling

Synthetic Data and Domain Randomization​

  1. Tobin, J., Fong, R., Ray, A., Schneider, J., Zaremba, W., & Abbeel, P. (2017). "Domain randomization for transferring deep neural networks from simulation to the real world." IROS 2017.

    • Techniques for transferring simulation-trained models to real robots
    • Critical for Physical AI sim-to-real transfer

  2. Handa, A., Patraucean, V., Pătrăucean, V., Kim, H., & Badamosi, A. (2019). "Synthia: A collection of synthetic datasets for scene understanding." Proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Workshops.

    • Synthetic data generation for perception tasks
    • Understanding how to create training data for Physical AI systems

Humanoid Robotics and Locomotion​

Bipedal Locomotion​

  1. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). "The 3D linear inverted pendulum mode: a simple modeling for a biped walking pattern generation." IROS 2003.

    • Foundational work on bipedal walking control
    • Essential for understanding humanoid balance and locomotion

  2. Pratt, J., Chew, C. M., Torres, A., & dilworth, P. (2001). "Virtual model control: obstacle avoidance for legged robots." Proceedings 2001 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.

    • Advanced approach to legged robot control
    • Understanding dynamic locomotion strategies

  3. Englsberger, J., Ott, C., & Peer, A. (2014). "Bipedal walking control based on Capture Point dynamics." IROS 2014.

    • Modern approach to humanoid walking control
    • Understanding Capture Point-based balance control

Whole-Body Control​

  1. Khatib, O., Park, H. J., Forrai, J., & Yokoi, K. (2004). "A unified operational space formulation for multi-task compliant control." IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation.

    • Advanced control framework for multi-task robotics
    • Critical for understanding whole-body humanoid control

  2. Stephens, B., & Atkeson, C. G. (2010). "Dynamic balance force control for compliant humanoid robots." 2010 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.

    • Force control for dynamic balance
    • Understanding how to maintain stability during movement

Machine Learning for Physical AI​

Reinforcement Learning in Robotics​

  1. Deisenroth, M. P., Fox, D., & Rasmussen, C. E. (2020). "Gaussian Processes for Machine Learning." MIT Press.

    • Essential for understanding uncertainty in robotics learning
    • Critical for safe learning in real-world systems

  2. Levine, S., Pastor, P., Krizhevsky, A., & Quillen, D. (2016). "Learning hand-eye coordination for robotic grasping with large-scale data collection." IJRR 2016.

    • Example of large-scale learning for manipulation
    • Understanding how to collect data for robot learning

  3. Tobin, J., Fong, R., Ray, A., Schneider, J., Zaremba, W., & Abbeel, P. (2017). "Domain randomization for transferring deep neural networks from simulation to the real world." IROS 2017.

    • Critical approach to sim-to-real transfer
    • Understanding how to train models in simulation for real-world use

Imitation Learning​

  1. Argall, B. D., Chernova, S., Veloso, M., & Browning, B. (2009). "A survey of robot learning from demonstration." Robotics and Autonomous Systems, 57(5), 469-483.

    • Comprehensive review of learning from demonstration
    • Essential for understanding how robots can acquire skills from humans

  2. Schaal, S. (1999). "Is imitation learning the route to humanoid robots?" Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 3(2), 63-71.

    • Philosophical and practical aspects of imitation learning
    • Understanding the role of imitation in humanoid robot development

Computer Vision for Physical AI​

Perception for Robotics​

  1. Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). "Probabilistic Robotics." MIT Press.

    • Comprehensive treatment of perception and navigation
    • Essential for understanding uncertainty in robotic perception

  2. Szeliski, R. (2010). "Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications." Springer.

    • Comprehensive computer vision reference
    • Understanding visual perception for Physical AI systems

  3. Mur-Artal, R., Montiel, J. M. M., & Tardós, J. D. (2015). "ORB-SLAM: a versatile and accurate monocular SLAM system." IEEE Transactions on Robotics.

    • State-of-the-art visual SLAM system
    • Understanding how robots can map and localize using vision

Social and Ethical Aspects of Humanoid Robotics​

Human-Robot Interaction​

  1. Breazeal, C. (2002). "Toward sociable robots." Robotics and Autonomous Systems, 42(3-4), 167-175.

    • Foundational work on social robotics
    • Understanding how humanoid robots can interact with humans

  2. Dautenhahn, K. (2007). "Socially intelligent robots: dimensions of human–robot interaction." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, 362(1480), 679-704.

    • Comprehensive review of social robotics concepts
    • Understanding the complexity of human-robot interaction

Ethics and Humanoid Robotics​

  1. Lin, P., Abney, K., & Bekey, G. A. (Eds.). (2011). "Robot ethics: mapping the issues for a mechanized world." MIT Press.
    • Ethical considerations in robotics design and deployment
    • Critical for responsible Physical AI development

Advanced Simulation and Tools​

NVIDIA Isaac and GPU-Accelerated Robotics​

  1. Isaac Sim Documentation. (2023). NVIDIA Developer. https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/isaacsim/

    • Official documentation for NVIDIA's high-fidelity simulation
    • Essential for advanced simulation workflows

  2. Isaac ROS Documentation. (2023). NVIDIA Developer. https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/

    • GPU-accelerated perception and navigation packages
    • Understanding hardware acceleration for Physical AI

Advanced Gazebo Usage​

  1. Gazebo Simulation Documentation. (2023). ODE. http://gazebosim.org/

    • Official Gazebo documentation
    • Understanding physics simulation for robotics

  2. Koenig, N., & Howard, A. (2004). "Design and use paradigms for Gazebo, an open-source multi-robot simulator." IROS 2004.

    • Foundational paper on Gazebo simulator design
    • Understanding the architecture of simulation systems

Research Papers and Journals​

Top Robotics Journals​

  1. The International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR) - Premier robotics research journal
  2. IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO) - High-impact robotics journal
  3. Autonomous Robots - Journal focusing on autonomous robotic systems
  4. Robotics and Autonomous Systems - Applied robotics research journal
  5. Frontiers in Robotics and AI - Open-access robotics journal with Physical AI focus

Key Conferences​

  1. IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) - Top robotics conference
  2. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS) - Premier robotics conference
  3. Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) - Top-tier robotics conference
  4. International Symposium on Robotics Research (ISRR) - Highly selective robotics research symposium

Online Resources and Communities​

Educational Resources​

  1. MIT 6.832 Underactuated Robotics (2023). Course materials available at MIT OpenCourseWare.

    • Advanced robotics course with focus on underactuated systems
    • Essential for understanding dynamic systems in robotics

  2. CMU 16-745 Optimal Control and Reinforcement Learning (2023).

    • Advanced course on optimal control for robotics
    • Understanding optimization-based robot control

  3. Stanford CS231A Computer Vision (2023).

    • Computer vision course with applications to robotics
    • Understanding visual perception for robotics

Active Research Groups​

  1. MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) - Robust Robotics Group

    • Leading research in robust planning and control for robots
    • Access to recent research papers and technical reports

  2. ETH Zurich Robotics Systems Lab

    • Research on legged robotics, aerial robotics, and autonomous systems
    • Technical reports on advanced robotics systems

  3. NVIDIA Research Robotics Group

    • GPU-accelerated robotics and AI research
    • Recent technical reports on Isaac Sim and Isaac ROS

Specialized Topics​

Manipulation and Grasping​

  1. Ott, C., Roa, M. A., & Hirzinger, G. (2015). "Decoupling-based control scheme for variable stiffness control of flexible joint robots." IEEE Transactions on Robotics.

    • Advanced control for robotic manipulation
    • Understanding variable impedance control

  2. Bohg, J., Morales, A., Asfour, T., & Kragic, D. (2010). "Data-driven grasp synthesis—A survey." IEEE Transactions on Robotics.

    • Comprehensive survey of robotic grasping approaches
    • Understanding how robots can grasp diverse objects

Multi-Robot Systems​

  1. Chen, J., & Zha, W. (2019). "Multi-robot path planning for non-holonomic systems." Annual Reviews in Control.

    • Advanced techniques for multi-robot coordination
    • Understanding how multiple robots can operate together

  2. Lewis, M. A., & Talukdar, T. N. (2000). "Heterarchical control for multi-robot systems." International Conference on Robotics and Automation.

    • Approaches to coordination in multi-robot systems
    • Understanding decentralized control strategies

Emerging Topics​

Neuromorphic Robotics​

  1. Indiveri, G., & Hsieh, Y. H. (2023). "Neuromorphic computing and robotics." Nature Machine Intelligence.
    • Next-generation computing for robotics applications
    • Understanding low-power, efficient robotic systems

Soft Robotics​

  1. Rus, D., & Tolley, M. T. (2015). "Design, fabrication and control of soft robots." Nature.
    • Emerging field of compliant, safe robotics
    • Understanding alternative approaches to robotics design

Key Takeaways​

  • This further reading list provides resources for deepening understanding across all aspects of Physical AI
  • Academic papers provide theoretical foundations and cutting-edge research
  • Books offer comprehensive treatments of specific topics
  • Online resources provide practical implementation guidance
  • Research groups and conferences keep you updated on the latest developments
  • Specialized topics address specific aspects of Physical AI development

Reading Strategy​

  1. Start with foundational texts to build strong theoretical understanding
  2. Move to application-focused papers to understand implementation
  3. Explore cutting-edge research in areas of special interest
  4. Engage with online communities for practical insights and networking
  5. Follow key researchers and institutions to stay current with developments
