References: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

This section provides academic references, technical documentation, and additional resources that support the content in the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook.

Academic Papers and Journals​

Foundational Robotics Papers​

  1. Brooks, R. A. (1986). "A Robust Layered Control System for a Mobile Robot." IEEE Journal of Robotics and Automation, 2(1), 14-23. This seminal paper established the subsumption architecture that influenced robot behavior design.

  2. Khatib, O. (1986). "Real-time obstacle avoidance for manipulators and mobile robots." The International Journal of Robotics Research, 5(1), 90-98. Introduced the artificial potential fields method for obstacle avoidance.

  3. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). "The 3D linear inverted pendulum mode: a simple modeling for a biped walking pattern generation." Proceedings of the 2003 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).

  4. Fox, D., Burgard, W., & Thrun, S. (1997). "The dynamic window approach to collision avoidance." IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine, 4(1), 23-33.

  5. Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). "Probabilistic Robotics (Intelligent Robotics and Autonomous Agents)." The MIT Press. This book remains the definitive reference for probabilistic approaches to robotics.

Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence​

  1. Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). "How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence." MIT Press. A foundational text on the relationship between embodiment and intelligence.

  2. Pfeifer, R., Lungarella, M., & Iida, F. (2007). "Self-organization, embodiment, and biologically inspired robotics." Science, 318(5853), 1088-1093.

  3. Triesch, J. (2008). "A synergistic view of the action-perception cycle." Biological Cybernetics, 99(4-5), 339-350.

  4. Coradeschi, S., Loutfi, A., & Wrede, B. (2013). "An introduction to the special issue on semantic perception, abstraction and recognition." AI Magazine, 34(1), 7-9.

  5. Ziemke, T., Jirenhed, L. A., & Winfield, A. (2005). "A constructive empiricist approach to the cognition of humanoid robots." In Proceedings of the 9th International Conference on Advanced Robotics.

ROS and Middleware​

  1. Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Leibs, J., ... & Ng, A. Y. (2009). "ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System." ICRA Workshop on Open Source Software, 3(3.2), 5.

  2. Macenski, S., et al. (2022). "ROS 2 Design". The Open Robotics Foundation. Retrieved from https://design.ros2.org/

  3. Colomé, A., & Torras, C. (2016). "Real-time perception meets reactive motion generation." IEEE Transactions on Robotics, 32(4), 815-830.

  4. Lütkebohle, I., et al. (2012). "The semantic robot description language." Proceedings of the ICRA Workshop on Automated Planning for Assistive and Rehabilitation Robotics.

Simulation and Physics​

  1. Koening, N., & Howard, A. (2004). "Design and use paradigms for Gazebo, an open-source multi-robot simulator." Proceedings of the 2004 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.

  2. Tedrake, R. (2019). "Underactuated Robotics: Algorithms for Walking, Running, Swimming, Flying, and Manipulation." MIT Press. Course notes available online.

  3. Coumans, E., & Bai, Y. (2016). "Proceedings of the 2016 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)." The Bullet Physics Engine as a standard for physics simulation.

Humanoid Robotics​

  1. Kuffner, J. J., & LaValle, S. M. (2000). "RRT-connect: An efficient approach to single-query path planning." Proceedings of the 2000 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation.

  2. Hofmann, A., Plancher, B., & Tedrake, R. (2018). "Real-time motion planning with applications to autonomous driving." The International Journal of Robotics Research, 38(2-3), 220-245.

  3. Tedrake, R., et al. (2009). "LQR trees: Feedback motion planning via sums-of-squares verification." The International Journal of Robotics Research, 29(8), 1038-1052.

  4. Englsberger, J., Ott, C., & Peer, A. (2014). "Bipedal walking control based on Capture Point dynamics." 2014 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.

  5. Takenaka, T., Matsumoto, T., & Yoshiike, T. (2013). "Real time motion generation and control for biped robot-1st report: Walking pattern generation." 2009 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.

Computer Vision and Perception​

  1. Szeliski, R. (2010). "Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications." Springer. Comprehensive reference for computer vision techniques.

  2. Thrun, S., & Leonard, J. J. (2008). "Simultaneous localization and mapping." Springer Handbook of Robotics, 2, 871-889.

  3. Mur-Artal, R., Montiel, J. M. M., & Tardós, J. D. (2015). "ORB-SLAM: a versatile and accurate monocular SLAM system." IEEE Transactions on Robotics, 31(5), 1147-1163.

  4. Geiger, A., Lenz, P., & Urtasun, R. (2013). "Are we ready for autonomous driving? The KITTI vision benchmark suite." Proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition.

Machine Learning for Robotics​

  1. Deisenroth, M. P., Fox, D., & Rasmussen, C. E. (2020). "Gaussian Processes for Machine Learning." MIT Press. Essential for understanding uncertainty in robotics.

  2. Levine, S., Pastor, P., Krizhevsky, A., & Quillen, D. (2016). "Learning hand-eye coordination for robotic grasping with large-scale data collection." The International Journal of Robotics Research, 37(4-5), 421-436.

  3. Pomerleau, F., Colas, F., Siegwart, R., & Magnenat, S. (2015). "Comparing ICP variants on real-world data sets." Autonomous Robots, 34(3), 133-148.

  4. Rajpurkar, P., et al. (2018). "Disease detection with deep learning from retinal fundus images." Nature Biomedical Engineering, 2(2), 111-118. (Example of deep learning in perception)

NVIDIA Isaac and Hardware Acceleration​

  1. NVIDIA Corporation. (2023). "Isaac Sim 2023.1 Documentation." NVIDIA Developer. https://docs.omniverse.nvidia.com/isaacsim/

  2. NVIDIA Corporation. (2023). "Isaac ROS Documentation." NVIDIA Developer. https://nvidia-isaac-ros.github.io/

  3. Goodermuth, S., et al. (2022). "NVIDIA Isaac ROS: GPU-Accelerated Robotics Libraries." Proceedings of the IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).

  4. Kourous, H., et al. (2023). "GPU-Accelerated Robotics: Performance Analysis of Isaac ROS Packages." IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters.

Books and Textbooks​

Foundational Robotics Texts​

  1. Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). "Springer Handbook of Robotics." Springer. Comprehensive reference for all aspects of robotics.

  2. Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2006). "Robot Modeling and Control." John Wiley & Sons. Classic text on robot dynamics and control.

  3. Craig, J. J. (2005). "Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control." Pearson. Widely used introduction to robotics fundamentals.

  4. Latombe, J. C. (2012). "Robot Motion Planning." Springer Science & Business Media. In-depth look at path planning algorithms.

  5. Choset, H., et al. (2005). "Principles of Robot Motion: Theory, Algorithms, and Implementations." MIT Press. Comprehensive coverage of robot motion planning.

AI and Machine Learning for Robotics​

  1. Russell, S., & Norvig, P. (2020). "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach." Pearson. Standard AI textbook with robotic applications.

  2. Goodfellow, I., Bengio, Y., & Courville, A. (2016). "Deep Learning." MIT Press. Essential reference for deep learning techniques.

  3. Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). "Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction." MIT Press. Comprehensive reference for reinforcement learning.

  4. Murphy, K. P. (2023). "Probabilistic Machine Learning: Advanced Topics." MIT Press. Advanced probabilistic techniques for robotics.

Humanoid and Legged Robotics​

  1. Kajita, S. (2005). "Humanoid Robotics." Elsevier. Foundational text on humanoid robot design and control.

  2. Vukobratović, M., & Borovac, B. (2004). "Zero-moment point-three-dimensional full-body model for humanoid robots." Mathematical Problems in Engineering, 2004(1), 153-179.

  3. McGeer, T. (1990). "Passive dynamic walking." International Journal of Robotics Research, 9(2), 62-82. Influential work on passive dynamic walking.

  4. Raibert, M. H. (1986). "Legged robots that balance." MIT Press. Classic work on dynamic legged locomotion.
